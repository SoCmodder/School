2.      Write a VHDL testbench to simulate the D flip-flop you created in Problem 3 of the last assignment.  The testbench should use assert statements to automatically tell you if the design works or where a problem occurs.  You should not have to force signals or look at waveforms in vsim to tell the result.  For an example of a testbench, see the class slides or look at the testbench designed for lab 2 at http://web.umr.edu/~daryl/classes/cpe311/F2002/ralu_tb.vhd.  Turn in a copy of your testbench.  I strongly recommend you try it out with vcom/vsim before turning it in.

3.      Design a VHDL controller for a stoplight.  The controller should have the following inputs/outputs:

·      red, yellow, green: OUT std_logic;

·      clk, walk: IN std_logic;

Under “normal” operation, the controller will cycle as: red for 255 counts (periods) of clk, green for 255 counts of clk, yellow for 31 counts of clk, and then back to red to start over again.  If someone pushes the walk button (signal walk goes high) and the stoplight is in the “green” state, it will immediately jump to the yellow state and, after the appropriate delay, then on to the red state.  It will only change states on the rising edge of walk.  Test your design with vcom/vsim.  Turn in a printout of a) your VHDL code and b) results of your test.
