CpE 311 Lab Exercise 1

Schematic Capture Based FPGA Design

Due Feb. 20, 2003

Purpose

The purpose of this exercise is to become familiar with schematic capture based design flow for Actel Field Programmable Gate Arrays (FPGA). Schematic capture has been the traditional design technique for many years. It is rapidly being replaced by HDL based design flows for FPGA design but is still commonly used for top level block diagrams and board level design for printed circuit layout. We will design a medium scale circuit using schematic capture in order to form a basis for comparison to HDL based design (which we’ll do in the next lab).

Problem Description

Arithmetic logic units (ALUs) are a major building block of most general and special purpose processors. An ALU, as the name implies, performs simple arithmetic operations like add and subtract and bit-wise logic operations like AND and OR on binary numbers of length 1, 4, 8, or larger. You are to design a (very simple) four bit ALU with an attached register and put it together in a single package.  The design will be implemented in an ACTEL 40MX02 FPGA.

 

Your ALU should have the following characteristics:

  • Inputs: A[3:0], B[3:0], S[1:0], and CI
  • Outputs: F[3:0], CO
  • Functions: F=0, A.or.B, A.xor.B, A.plus.B.plus.CI, where S=0 selects F=0, S=1 selects F=A.or.B, and so forth through S=3 which selects A+B+CI.
  • A title block in the bottom right hand corner of your design with a title for your project (e.g. CpE311 Lab Exercise 1 - Actel ALU design), your name and the date of the design.

A and B are the four bit input operands with bit 0 as the LSB. CI is the carry in for arithmetic operations and is ignored for logic operations. CO is the carry out for arithmetic operations. S is the function select.  You may consider the inputs to be unsigned binary numbers. 

When your ALU is complete, make a symbol for it and then package it together with an accumulator register (ACC) and carry flip flop (CY). Your registered ALU should have the following characteristics:

·      Inputs: B[3:0], S[1:0], sysclk (system clock), RST

·      Outputs: F[3:0], CY

·      All flip flops should be clocked on the rising edge of the same system clock (sysclk). Don't forget to use the 40MX02's clock buffer.

·      The F output of the ALU should be connected to the ACC's input and the ACC output to the ALU A input.

·      The ALU carry out bit (CO) should be connected to the CY input and the CY output to the ALU carry input (CI).

·      The CY should only change during arithmetic operations. In other words, operations like AND should not affect the carry bit.

·      A title block in the bottom right hand corner of your design with a title for your project, your name and the date of the design.

The B input, sysclk, and S inputs are primary chip inputs. ACC (F[3:0]) and CY are primary outputs. An additional RST chip input should be provided to enable resetting the flip flops when power is first applied. RST should be active high.

Implement your registered ALU with an Actel 40MX02 FPGA. Make a reasonable pin assignment for the PLC44 package. Use the A40MX02-3 PL44 part (commercial temperature range). Estimate the number of Actel logic modules used before synthesizing your design with designer (preferably before even capturing your design schematic). Send me the estimated number of logic elements and I/O modules. Perform a functional (pre-route) and timing (post-route) simulation to test your design appropriately. Keep a log of all time spent on this lab assignment.

Some Hints

·      You can find design hints for developing your design for an Actel part at: http://web.umr.edu/~daryl/classes/cpe311/actel.notes.txt.  You’re interested in the part under the heading “Actel Design Flow – Schematic Capture” and “Brief outline of actel place and route procedure”.

·      You can find the Actel libraries under the menu Libraries>Actel Libraries. There are MANY parts available in the Actel 40MX02 library. You may find the following parts very useful in your design: FA1, CLKBUF, OUTBUF, INBUF, MX2, MX4, GND, DFC1

·      Seeing all the parts in the library is easier if you enable the scroll bars.  Place the mouse cursor in the library window, hit the right mouse button, and hit “enable scroll bars”

·      You need to use OUTBUF and INBUF (or TRIBUF) components in the Actel library to connect to pins (going to the outside world).  OUTBUF and INBUFs should be connected to portin and portout flags named with the appropriate pin (e.g. p32).  The buffers tell the tool to connect to a pin.  The port flags tell the tool what to name the port.

OUTBUF connected to a portout flag.  The flag wasn’t numbered with a pin number in this case.

·      Think carefully about your test plan – this is an important part of your result.  A test should “exercise” your circuit to see if it works correctly, though you may not have the time to run through all possible combinations and check all results.  In that case, try to check cases you think might be important (for example, perform and operation that generates a carry as a 1 and one with carry as 0.  Test that carry is only latched on an arithmetic operation, etc).

·      The Actel 40MX02 FPGA uses the same basic logic module as the ACT1.  It does not have a sequential module.

·      You can connect wires “virtually” by simply giving them the same name.  DA will perform the connections behind the scenes.

·      You can create a symbol for your ALU by completing the following steps: 1) check and save your sheet, 2) select miscellaneous-create symbol from the main menu, and 3) select OK to create symbol.  Once you have created your symbol, you can start editing your registered ALU by 1) opening a new sheet and 2) selecting “choose symbol”.
