Homework Problem 1
Tentatively due: Thur., Jan. 30.

  1. You’re evaluating the cost of a full custom design. Assume the following:

·     The cost of each wafer is $2000

·     You can fit approximately 200 dies on each wafer

·     Each die has an area of approximately 1cm2

·     The defect rate is approximately 0.5 defects/cm2

·     the parameter alpha in the die yield equation is 3.

·     cost of packaging is $0.10 per part

·     cost of die test is $0.05 per part

·     final test yield is 90% (0.90)

 

a)      What is the current die yield (i.e. how many dies are good out of the 200 on each wafer)?

b)      What is the cost of each die (piece of silicon that will go in each chip)?

c)      What is the total cost per part?

d)      If we increase the size of each chip to 2cm on a side (an area of approximately 4cm2) and then get about 40 dies on each wafer, what will the die yield be (i.e. how many dies are good out of the 40 on each wafer)?

e)      If start-up costs for this full custom design (the one with 1cm2 area) is $500,000 and you can fit your same design into an FPGA that costs $50/part, how many parts would you have to sell to make the full-custom approach economically feasible?

  1. Choosing a chip technology. Say you are asked to design an ASIC for a high-tech product with a short lifespan and a small total production (i.e. 1000s of units, not 10,000s of units). It is expected that the ASIC will NOT contain a particularly large number of gates and will NOT have to be particularly fast. Explain what type of chip technology (custom, CBIC, MGA, FPGA, etc.) you believe would be best for this project. Defend your selection, such that you convince me your choice is right and that you thoroughly understand your options.  Be sure to mention the capabilities of each technology and the economics related to your decision.
  2. Perform the Mentor Graphics Tutorial at http://web.umr.edu/~daryl/classes/cpe311/W2003/mentor_tutorial_1a.pdf. Write down each step you performed and a brief description of what it did (e.g. 1. entered "sul" and "swd" at the command prompt -- set the working directory and library. 2. typed "da" -- started design architect). If a step was repeated, you do not have to list it a second time. You will find this list useful in future labs.

You may mostly skip over the sections on Bold Browser and on Design Manager, as these tools are not used so much anymore. Also: the logging in procedure has been simplified since this tutorial was written. The only thing you really have to do now before starting Mentor Graphics is to a) create a directory you will use for this assignment, b) cd to that directory, then c) type 'sul' (return) and 'swd' (return). These last two Unix commands will set the Mentor Graphics environment variables for the working directory and user library.
