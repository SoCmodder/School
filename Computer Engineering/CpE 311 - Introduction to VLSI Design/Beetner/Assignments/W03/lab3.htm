CpE 311 Lab Exercise 3

Transistor Level Cell Design

Due April 1, 2003

Purpose

Familiarization with transistor level cell design using Spice.

Problem Description

1. Work the Accusim tutorial available on my webpage at http://www.umr.edu/~daryl/classes/cpe311/daacsim v2.pdf.  The first part of this tutorial looks very much like what you did in lab 1.  In addition to what is asked for in the lab:

·      Measure the input capacitance of port A1 before and after the change in size of M101.  The capacitance of port A1 in part 1 can be approximated using the relationship I = C dv/dt.  To measure current going into the gate of a transistor, select the gate node (not the wire! – you will only see a little crosshair showing the selected node) and hit trace.  Be careful in your selection of the portion of the V and I graphs you use to calculate your result.  There is a tool available for measuring average values through integration.

·      Measure TpHL and TpLH from both A1 and B1 to Out and describe the process. 

·      Measure the input capacitance of port A1 both when Vcc=5v and Vcc=3.3v.

·      In addition to the above, turn in the schematics and simulation output.

2. Develop a transistor level schematic similar to the one shown in Fig 2.13 of the text but for an OAI21X2 gate.  Use the methodology developed in the tutorial to simulate your gate.  Use the modelfile t1ck, which is a model from a recent MOSIS run using the AMI 0.5 micron process.  For this modelfile to work, your transistors should use model “n” (not cmosn) for the NFET and “p” for the PFET (look at the file and see why…). Assume a load capacitance of 1 pF.  Make the transistors as small as possible consistent with equal worst-case rise and fall times.

3. Repeat part 2 but assume a load capacitance of 10 pF.

Hints

·     You may not get your delays, etc, to come out exactly as reported in the tutorial, but they should be close.

·     In part 2, remember that you want to make the worst case rise and fall times approximately the same.  It is virtually impossible to make them identical.

Report

Your report should include:

·      A cover page with the title Lab Assignment No. 3, Transistor Level Cell Design, your names, the date, and CPE311. 

·      All schematics and simulation output from part 1. 

·      Measured input capacitance to port A1 before and after size change. 

·      Measured values of TpHL and TpLH for A1 and B1 to Out.

·      A hardcopy of the OAI21X2 gate schematic and a plot of the transient solution input and output voltage waveforms for parts 2 and 3.

Do not use any form of binder or protector for your report.  Staple in the upper left corner only.  Your report should not exceed 10 pages maximum (not including simulation plots).
